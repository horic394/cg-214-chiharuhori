Dogo Onsen in Japan

Traditional bathing moments

Japan is very famous for hot springs which is contributed by its geographical factors. There are many hot spring spots around the nation, but I'd like to introduce Dogo Onsen (道後温泉/ Onsen means hot spring) in Ehime. Ehime is located in Shikoku area which is a separated western island.

Dogo onsen is one of the oldest hot spring which was mentioned in Nihon-shoki, the oldest historical book. The building itself was rebuilt many times and current one was built in 1890.

Inside of building is very traditional Japanese style. Flat tatami, wood columns and windows made by Japanese paper. Before taking bath, everyone visit this common space to get each own Yukata, Japanese traditional casual kimono.

They have 2 types of bathrooms, Kami-no-Yu and Tama-no-Yu. Each room is for women/men depending on the day. This is Kami-no-Yu. I remember the temperature was high so that I couldn't stay in the water long. But the inside atmosphere was very special.

Japan is the eastern Asian country of many islands. The population is 126,000,000.
