Iceland

Natural bathing experience

Iceland attracts many tourists by its unique natural scenery and hot springs. Tourists can enjoy both organic hot springs and artificial hot springs. If you want to go to natural ones, you should go to a road trip which literally goes around the nation. On the way to the trip, you'll find hidden hot springs.This is Grjotagja Thermal Spring in North Iceland.

The most popular hot spring is Blue Lagoon, which is very close to Reykjavik. This is an artificial hot spring which uses wasted heat after domestic water. Clay deep in the water is good for skin. There are colder part and warmer part in the water, sometimes it's annoying. But overall, I can't complain it because the blue water color is really amazing!

But I liked local swimming pools around the city. There are hot tabs outside and local people come and gather with their families and friends under stars at night. Some swimming pools have sliders and other attractions. If you are lucky, you can see the northern lights while taking a bath! The price is very cheaper than bluelagoon, about $8. So, if you want to try something very local and save your budget, local swimming pools worth trying.

Iceland is one of European countries which exists between Europe and American continent. It has 330,000 population and one third of Icelandic people live around the capital city, Rejkyavik. Iceland is famous for viking and fishing. There are many cool artists such as Bjork and Sigur Ros. New wave artists are also coming up and they worth checking out!
